What this is
Every pattern has a thread.
This tool helps you
find yours.
A situation happens. Something inside you responds. You call the connection between them obvious — but it isn't. It was assigned. And you've been assigning the same connection, in different rooms, with different people, for a long time.

The Thread is a log. Every time something activates in your system, you enter three things: what happened externally (Variable A), what you experienced internally (Variable B), and the connection you assigned between them. Over time, the pattern becomes visible in your own entries — not because the tool interpreted it, but because you wrote it down.
The Variable Viewer
Example entry logged today
A
Meeting got cancelled with no explanation
B
Felt invisible — like what I bring doesn't register
→
When things get cancelled, it means I don't matter
// The connection between A and B was assigned — not given. That assignment is the data.
The situation didn't create the experience. The connection you assigned between the situation and the experience created it. That connection is a formation — and it has been running longer than this room.
Why it works over time
01
First entry — you log what happened
One situation. One internal state. One assigned connection. Thirty seconds. The entry is logged with a timestamp.
02
Over days — entries accumulate
Different situations. Different rooms. But if the same connection keeps appearing in the → column — that is the pattern. Not one event. The recurring assignment.
03
The thread becomes visible
You don't need someone to tell you what your pattern is. Your own entries show you. The Thread doesn't interpret — it reflects your log back to you in sequence.
04
Pattern recognition changes the gap
When you can see the assignment before you make it — the gap between A and B opens. That gap is where a different connection becomes possible.
