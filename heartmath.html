The Science

The heart is not
a pump with feelings.

The heart generates the largest electromagnetic field in the body — 60 times greater in amplitude than the brain. It sends more signals up to the brain than it receives down from it. It has its own nervous system. It is not responding to your emotional state. It is participating in creating it.

Heart Rate Variability — the variation in time between each heartbeat — is the clearest window into nervous system regulation we currently have. High HRV indicates flexibility, resilience, and the capacity to self-regulate. Low HRV indicates chronic stress and a system running on override.

What HRV actually measures

A perfectly metronomic heartbeat is not a sign of health — it's a sign of rigidity. The variation between beats reflects your autonomic nervous system making real-time adjustments. Wide, rhythmic variation (coherence) means your system is adaptable. Erratic or suppressed variation means something is running on override. Coherence training makes that pattern visible, then teachable.

What Training Looks Like

01

Baseline reading

A pulse sensor connects to the HeartMath device. Within seconds, your HRV pattern appears on screen — the actual rhythm your heart has been running. Not estimated. Not averaged from a week of data. Right now, in this moment, in this room.

02

Observe the incoherence

Before training begins, you see what stress, rumination, or suppression actually looks like as data. Jagged, irregular patterns. The nervous system doing what it's been doing — usually for years. Seeing it reflected back is often its own kind of shift.

03

The coherence technique

A specific breathing rhythm combined with a focused positive emotion (not performed — recalled) produces coherence: smooth, sine-wave-like HRV patterns that indicate the heart and brain are synchronizing. The device shows the shift in real time as it happens. You learn what coherence feels like in your body by watching it appear on the screen.

04

Practice builds the skill

Coherence is not a permanent state — it's a skill. The more you enter it deliberately, the more accessible it becomes under pressure. Over sessions, the baseline HRV pattern shifts. The system that was running on override starts to have more range.

05

Integration with pattern work

A coherent state makes the nervous system receptive rather than defended. Entering coherence before a hypnosis session — or after framework mapping — changes the quality of what's possible in that work. The tools are designed to be used together.
